La Routa:
Adventure Racing World Series - Asia Regional Championships

3rd Week, November 2025 / Philippines

The La Routa Adventure Race is the longest-running adventure race in the Philippines. First held annually from 2000 to 2010, it returned for a revival race in 2019 and became part of the Adventure Racing World Series (ARWS) Asia Circuit in 2023, hosting the first Asian Championship for ARWS.

La Routa is an expedition-style, four-day adventure race covering approximately 350 kilometers. Navigation is key—teams of four must find a series of control points using only a map and compass, with a non-standard, user-specified map scale. Teams are free to choose their routes, but must reach all control points in the correct sequence.

The race features trekking, mountain biking, ocean swimming, paddling, high elements, and ropework, with traditional Filipino games thrown in for a cultural and strategic twist. The course spans paved roads, remote villages, winding single tracks, and dense tropical jungle, testing endurance, teamwork, and mental resilience.

In La Routa, the race itself determines your category. There are three classifications, based on performance at specific cut-offs:

  1. Elite – The top-performing teams who complete the full course.
  2. Candidates – Teams meeting the second cut-off but missing the Elite mark.
  3. Aspirants – Teams finishing before the first cut-off but not qualifying for Candidates.
